Important Constitutional Amendments of India

Quick Revision Sheet for AIIMS & Competitive Exams

Key Amendments (Amendment + Year + Key Point)

Amendment Year Key Point
1st Amendment 1951 Added 9th Schedule; limited freedom of speech; protected land reforms.
7th Amendment 1956 Reorganisation of States; abolished Part B states.
24th Amendment 1971 Explicitly empowered Parliament to amend Fundamental Rights.
25th Amendment 1971 Curtailed right to property; introduced Article 31C.
31st Amendment 1973 Increased Lok Sabha seats to reflect population growth.
42nd Amendment 1976 Called “Mini Constitution”; added Socialist, Secular, Integrity; introduced Fundamental Duties.
44th Amendment 1978 Right to Property removed as Fundamental Right; restored civil liberties after Emergency.
52nd Amendment 1985 Anti-defection law added (Tenth Schedule).
61st Amendment 1989 Lowered voting age from 21 to 18 years.
69th Amendment 1991 Delhi made National Capital Territory with special status.
73rd Amendment 1992 Gave constitutional status to Panchayati Raj institutions.
74th Amendment 1992 Strengthened Urban Local Bodies (Municipalities).
86th Amendment 2002 Made Right to Education (6–14 years) a Fundamental Right (Article 21A).
101st Amendment 2016 Introduced Goods and Services Tax (GST).
